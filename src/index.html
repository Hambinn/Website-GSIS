About Us


Ganesha Student Innovation Summit (GSIS) is a national forum of discussion for college students to learn about the real challenges facing Indonesia today, in the perspective of business, art, and technology. GSIS aims to encourage collaboration and innovation as a drive for a better and more progressive society. GSIS starts out with a series of pre-events then peaked with the main event of online conference and hands-on challenge.

GSIS was born from the basis of realization that cross-expertise, 21st century collaboration breeds some of the biggest innovations that transform humanity today.

Social platforms, marketplaces, consumer electronics — things we most use now are mostly the outcome of vital integration between design, business, and technology; that is why GSIS try to focus on these 3 fields. The sweet intersection between these 3 can unlock new ways to drive positive change for the society and environment.

And for that, we hope that YOU as future GSIS delegates will collaborate, and rethink, reimagine, and reapply your skills & knowledge that you get from GSIS to a broad spectrum of industries — while in the long term, we hope that you will pass on this spirit to creating more positive impact for your surroundings.

Hope to see you in GSIS 2022!

Warm regards,

Palita Rana Putinagari, Founder & Executive Director GSIS 2020

Goals

Mission

  • Providing informative & insightful sessions where students can learn and identify real actual problems.
  • Diversifying the expertise areas to strengthen multidisciplinary teamwork.
  • Providing a collaboration opportunity where students can apply their expertise and learning materials to produce innovative solutions

Vision

To foster an environment where students nation-wide can spark innovative ideas to solve problems around them.

Values

Pre-Event

Webinar Series

The first event is the Webinar Series. With the topic of "How does an industry sustain in the post-pandemic era?", it will discuss the condition of the speaker’s industry in this post-pandemic era, including the changes implemented to help their industry sustain in this disruptive era. The chosen industry will be in the fields of technology, business, or the arts. In maintaining the stability of the speaker's industry, it can also be related to how effective the innovations are for this matter, also their plans for the future ahead, so that the participants of this webinar will gain insights into the innovations carried out by various industries.

Innovation Talk (Live)

Because of the pandemic our lives are facing a constant change, forcing us to adapt and adjust through all sorts of transformation. Innovation Talk, that will be held on Instagram live, will discuss how companies and brands implement their idea of adjustment through innovations. The product of each innovation will be discussed further, especially regarding the public's response to the product and how it transforms our way of life. In addition, it will also discuss how this innovation will thrive not only for now, but also in the upcoming and uncertain future. It is hoped that with this theme, the participants can get new ideas to produce innovative products that are relevant in the future. 

IG Takeover

This Instagram Takeover will discuss the daily routines of the speaker who works in the fields of technology, business, and the arts in this post-pandemic era. The competences that are needed and tips & tricks for the speaker’s position in the institution would be talked about in this Instagram Takeover. In addition, before the Instagram Takeover is carried out, a survey will be conducted regarding what Ganesha Student Innovation Summit’s Instagram viewers want to know through the question box so that it is hoped that this Instagram Takeover can answer the curiosity of Ganesha Student Innovation Summit Instagram viewers regarding related sources. With this Instagram Takeover, we’ll get a sneak peek in the life of professionals in various industries.

Main Event

National Conference

GSIS College Conference is the Main Event of Ganesha Student Innovation Summit. Various speakers who are  experts in their field which are technology, art, and business will gather to discuss and share their thoughts regarding how an innovation can impact people?, giving new perspectives to the general public and also a platform for them to explore and learn through some challenges. Therefore, encouraging students to create, collaborate, and innovate for the greater good.

Individual Challenge

The individual challenge is the first of a series of challenges that will take place in GSIS 2022. Each participant will be given a case based on their expertise, which will be determined during the registration process by selecting a stream. Stream’s expertise is divided into three areas: business, technology, and art. Participants are expected to be able to solve the issues given by using a collaborative approach, as well as develop ideas that are both applicable and relevant to the grand theme.

Team-Up

At this stage, 45 participants will be chosen to advance to the Team-Up Challenge round. The chosen participants will then be divided into 15 groups of three, with each member representing a different stream (1 technology, 1 business, and 1 art). Each stream will then be given a case that must be solved using a diverse-yet-collaborative approach from the perspectives of those three different areas. Later on, there will be a debate session consisting of three different teams, and each team will have to prove their solution by building arguments during the debate session.

Individual Challenge

Why should I participate in the GSIS Individual challenge?

This challenge offers you an excellent opportunity to exercise your analytical and theoretical knowledge in a fun and exciting way based on your respective stream. You will be able to ideate your own innovation based on the case that will be given. You may also meet some of the most successful and influential people in the business, art , and technology world.

Will I be able to register for more than one stream?

No, all participants must choose one stream as their area of expertise in order to join up in the Individual Challenge session, but participants may join all of the streams in the entire college conferences with no boundaries.

Is there any cost to enter the GSIS Individual Challenge?

Yes! There will be extra fees in addition for participants for joining the GSIS Individual Challenge. Participants will be required to pay for the package that includes access to the college conference and the GSIS Individual Challenge. 

Is it necessary to be nominated by my university/college before submitting my entry?

No, You don’t need to be nominated by your institution in order to join the Individual Challenge. However, we may request proof of whether you are a full-time undergraduate business school student in either a public or private university in Indonesia during the registration.

Apart from undergraduates attending full-time degree courses, can students attending associate degree/diploma/certificate courses enter the competition?

No, the competition is only for full-time undergraduate students. Full-time postgraduate students from local universities, colleges or institutes of vocational education, etc. are not eligible to enter the competition.

Is it mandatory to attend the college conference arranged by the organizer? Will I be disqualified if I cannot attend all or any of them?

No, it is not compulsory for you to attend the college conference, and you will not be disqualified for not attending. However, you might miss a lot of useful information, key take points, and insights. So it is recommended that participants be present at least during their respective stream college conference.

Team-Up Challenge

What is the composition of each team?

Each team will be composed of three, currently enrolled, undergraduate students . Students from different streams (business, art, and technology)will be mixed and formed into an entirely new team for the Team-Up Challenge. Students will be connected prior to the competition through web groups arranged by GSIS Academics Management Team.

What is the length and nature of the case that will be analyzed in the Team-Up Challenge Session?

The nature of the case that will be used, and the particular business issues involved, will be kept secret until competition packets are distributed 24 hours before the final stage of the competition. The case will still be correlated to the case that was given during the individual challenge session. Teams are expected to combine the knowledge, insights, and expertise throughout their respective stream to create an ultimate evolutionary ideation and innovation to solve the case

Can teams use other consultants throughout the GSIS 2022  case competition?

No. Teams are expected to produce original work solely attributable to the three members. Teams may not consult with advisors, other faculty, staff, team hosts, other students, or any other non-team members during the preparation of the case analysis or on the day of competition. Students are welcome to use books, internet web page information, or any other non-human resource to gather information

Any other helpful hints in regard to Team-Up Challenge preparation?

It is always an advantage to be educated about current world issues and the corresponding insights and knowledge about the GSIS 2022 grand theme. Teams should become familiar with conducting online searches for relevant information and sources.

How will the final stage (Team-Up Challenge)  be managed?

24 hours before the final presentation stage, participants will receive the case that needs to be solved by the team and needs to submit it before midnight of the final presentation day. The next morning, all of the finalists need to present their work in front of the judges. Each presentation will include  5 minutes for presentation, followed by a 5-minute question-and-answer session (with a panel of judges).
 
In addition to that, there will be a debate session, where the GSIS Academic Management team has previously formed five debate groups where each group will consist of three different teams.

How should the presentation session be delivered by the finalist?

Teams may present their case analysis using PowerPoint. Teams should always be prepared for the unexpected and have a back-up plan, i.e. if the laptop fails, you may want to have alternate plans. Team members should wear attire typical to a formal business presentation. We highly recommend that the presentation of the case solution include all team members, and be as “balanced” as possible. Teams should work to capitalize on the strengths of each individual team member!

How should the QnA session be prepared by the finalist?

Teams should be prepared for questions from the judges. The judges may opt to assume roles during the Q&A — for example, board members, firm officers, executives, etc.

How will the debate session be managed?

Each team must bring their arguments and challenge the other team's solution to the case. The jury may throw a topic to further analyze the team’s understanding of the case. Each team will have a total of three minutes of speaking time. So to speak, that one debate session will be 9 minutes long.

How many judges/juries are there during the final stage of the GSIS Competition?

There will be 3-4 judges  composed of corporate judges to assess and evaluate throughout the final stage, the Presentation stage,, and also the debate session.

How will the judging work?

Judging will be “blind”, meaning our judges will not be told and should not be led by the team to know which school each student represents. Teams should not include any graphics or words in the presentation that would identify your school , and clothing should be neutral in color and not school-oriented in any way.
